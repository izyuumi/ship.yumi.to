Sound familiar?

If you're doing tax work, you've lived this story.

📧

The copy-paste email loop

You write the same "Hi [Name], just following up on those documents…" email forty times a week. Then half your clients don't respond. So you do it again.

📊

The tracking spreadsheet from hell

You've got a tab in Excel tracking who sent what. It's 30 clients wide. You update it manually. It's always slightly out of date. You're never confident.

📁

Documents scattered everywhere

Some clients email attachments. Some text you photos. Some use Dropbox. One guy faxed something. Before you do any tax work, you spend an hour just organizing.

📞

The "did you get my stuff?" calls

Clients are anxious too. They uploaded something to Dropbox three weeks ago. Did you see it? They call to ask. You have to go look. It wasn't what you needed anyway.

Here's how GatherFlow fixes it

A focused tool that handles the document collection side of your practice — so you can spend your time on the work only you can do.

1

Build your client's document checklist

Select what documents each client needs: W-2s, 1099s, mortgage interest, charitable receipts, K-1s, and more. GatherFlow has a library of common tax documents — or you can add custom items. Set it once and reuse each year.

2

Send your client a secure upload link

GatherFlow generates a branded, secure portal for each client. One click to send. Clients see exactly what they need to upload — no login required, works on any device. They upload, you get notified instantly.

3

Automatic reminders do the follow-up for you

Set a reminder schedule (Day 3, 7, 14, 21…) and GatherFlow sends polite, professional nudges automatically until all documents arrive. The emails come from your name. You write the template once. The chasing happens without you.

4

See every client's status at a glance

Your dashboard shows every client: complete, in progress, or stuck. See exactly which documents are missing for each client. Know at a glance who's ready to work on — without digging through email.
